Outcomes of Riot: League of Legends Patch Changes (10.18-10.19)

In Riot: League of Legends, there are 151 playable champions. There are 5 champions picked for each team and 5 champions banned for each team. Champions can only be picked 1 time per game played among 2 teams. Champions can be banned 1 time per team per game which can add up to 2 times per game. Some champions are stronger than others which leads to a variety of win-rates, pick-rates, and ban-rates among them.

When champions become too strong or are too weak, Riot makes patch changes to attempt to balance champion statistics across all skill levels. These patch changes reflect differing win-rates among the champions affected. Champions that receive an increase, or “buff”, to their statistics are expected to have a higher win-rate percentage than the previous patch and vice versa to champions who receive a decrease, or “nerf”, to their stats. These win-rates are reflective of their patch changes, pick-rates, and total games played.

Buffed & Nerfed Champion's Win Rate for Patch 10.18 & 10.19

Win-rate is the first statistic to consider if the champion’s buff or nerf was actually affected as this reflects in the gameplay. A champion experiencing a buff (blue dot) should see an increase in win-rate while a nerf (red dot) should cause a decrease in win-rate. One champion’s (Udyr) win-rate appears to drop due to an increase in total games played. This can be explained by a lack of experience in players attempting to play the champion simply because they received a buff.

Buffed & Nerfed Champion's Pick Rate for Patch 10.18 & 10.19

Pick-rate is an important statistic to consider as it reflects the communities opinion on if a character has increased in viability in the meta. A champion experiencing a buff should see an increase in pick-rate while a nerf should cause a decrease in pick-rate. The nerfed champion (Fiora) that had an opposite effect compared to what was expected had negligible change and could be attributed to that champion’s higher total number of games.

Buffed & Nerfed Champion's Games Played for Patch 10.18 & 10.19

The total number of games played is an important factor regarding the players’ thoughts on the patch changes. The new meta spawns a change in total games played based on the buff which results in a higher expected number of total games played compared to a nerf that expects a lower total number of games played. The nerfed champion (Fiora) that had an opposite effect compared to what was expected displayed a higher pick-rate as well compared to the patch before her nerf.

Conclusions

In conclusion, the statistic to pay close attention to would be the win-rate. This may be an area where a champion has received too much of a buff or too much of a nerf. However, these also may be areas where the inconsistency of players alter the statistics. As stated earlier, Udyr’s win-rate drops due to an increase in total games played.
